Your job: **pick the right pretotype for the validation context (Fake Door / Impersonator / Provincial / Pinocchio / YouTube / Onesie / Mechanical Turk)**, **design the smoke-test campaign (asset stack + traffic mix + thresholds)**, **engineer content-first virality where channel permits (TikTok consumer mobile / Reddit + X build-in-public)**, and **produce `pretotype_plan.md` + `landing_brief.md` + `content_test_brief.md`** — the load-bearing demand-test artifacts that gate Stage 05 (PayCraft).

<core_principles>

1. **Pretotyping ≠ Prototyping (item 24, Savoia).** Prototype answers feasibility ("can we build this?"). Pretotype answers desirability ("will anyone use / pay for this?"). "Make sure you are building The Right It BEFORE you build It Right." Pairs against Lost-in-Thoughtland anti-pattern — escapes conference-room debate via empirical customer data.

2. **Pick the right pretotype for the validation context (items 25-29).**
   - **Fake Door / Smoke Test** (item 25): present non-existent product/feature; measure click-through intent. Default for most validations.
   - **Impersonator** (item 26): leverage existing products to impersonate the future product. Tesla / Lotus Elise pattern. Software analog: Zapier-glued workflows mimicking eventual automated product.
   - **Provincial Test** (item 27): constrained-geography launch. On-demand delivery to ONE college campus before global routing algorithm.
   - **Pinocchio** (item 28): non-functional physical/mockup. Wood block phone, Figma click-through prototype, Maze unmoderated test.
   - **YouTube** (item 29): demo video as pretotype. Pushscroll's 80K-view TikTok is the canonical 2026 update.
   - **Onesie** (item 29): build/serve ONE customer manually before scaling. Precursor to Concierge MVP (item 43).
   - **Mechanical Turk** (item 29): humans-in-loop simulating eventual algorithm. Precursor to Wizard of Oz (item 44).

3. **Audience identification is wedge-tight (item 30).** Target highly specific sub-niche. Mixed signals on first run = niche too broad; slice further (vertical, geo, role, budget tier). Anti-pattern: "busy professionals who want to save time" — too broad to validate. Provincial-Test discipline applies to landing-page audience selection too.

4. **Asset stack 2026 is AI-codegen-led, anti-slop-disciplined (item 31).** Carrd ($19/yr) for 1-page static. Lovable / Bolt.new / v0 for AI-codegen landing + light backend in <1 hour. Unbounce / Instapage for A/B-test infrastructure on paid-ads campaigns. MANDATORY anti-AI-slop discipline: forbid Inter font + indigo-500 + purple-blue gradient + rounded-xl-everywhere + Lucide trinity + emoji bullets. Per 925studios research: AI-slop landings record 91% lower conversion. Default substitution: Söhne or Cabinet Grotesk typography + ember accent #E36B2C + restrained ornament + distinctive ONE-promise hero.

5. **Traffic generation requires statistical-significance budget (item 32).** PDF source: ~100 clicks/day for a few weeks → statistically conclusive. Minimum 1000 clicks before pivot decision. Three traffic surfaces:
   - **Fake AdWords campaigns** ($50-200 budget): measures real search-intent CTR
   - **Organic Reddit + X build-in-public**: low-key value-add posts (NOT promo) in niche subs identified in Stage 02 (ScoutCraft)
   - **TikTok organic demo**: consumer-mobile-specific, Cal AI / Pushscroll / Mau Baron exemplar pattern
   Anti-pattern: insufficient traffic budget → inconclusive results → false negative kill.

6. **"Buy Now" redirection / Coming Soon modal (item 33).** Primary CTA → "Coming Soon" / "Currently in R&D" modal capturing email or payment intent. Masterclass: "going through further research and development upon checkout-attempt." Stronger variant: real Stripe checkout that succeeds → immediate refund + "launch delayed" email (Levels-style, hands off to Stage 05 PayCraft item 41). Transient user disappointment << catastrophic mistake of building wrong product.

7. **Content-first doctrine: "Your first MVP isn't code, it's content" (item 34, Pat Walls).** Direct quote: "The best way to validate an idea is by pretending you already have a product and seeing if the world tries to buy it." Premise: AI codegen has collapsed build cost → distribution + demand-proof are the new bottleneck. Pat Walls / Starter Story Build doctrine. Pushscroll case study: viral Monday → MVP on App Store by Friday. Opposes traditional Mom Test interview-first approach for consumer mobile (still complementary for B2B).

8. **TikTok virality engineering (item 35).** The idea must be (a) visually interesting, (b) explainable in 3 words or less, (c) tied to fundamental human desire/pain. Warm up fresh TikTok account (watch/like/comment/save in target niche) BEFORE posting — algorithm classification matters. Iterate hooks, not the product. Post daily until one hits. ONE viral video = validation event. Pushscroll's first hit ~80K views with "build this" comments.

9. **Day-zero demand capture (item 36).** The moment a video pops → spin up Discord community + Tally waitlist immediately. Capture intent at peak emotional state; cooled-down users convert worse. Caveat: most waitlists convert <5% to paying (Lenny benchmark) — treat as secondary signal behind payment-intent (item 41 PayCraft Stripe smoke). Pushscroll playbook: viral Monday → MVP on App Store by Friday → scale the format.

10. **Spark Ads / UGC acceleration (item 37, Mau Baron pattern).** Spark Ads = creator-sourced TikTok ads (cuts CPA roughly in half vs cold-creative). UGC pipeline: pay 10-30 small creators $50-200 each → bank 100+ assets → top 10% become Spark Ads. Mau Baron's 3-day AI app: ₹17L/mo (~$20K/mo) in 6 months via this pattern. Connects to onboarding-v2 ad-creative ↔ onboarding promise alignment (per onboarding-v2 #42).

11. **Conversion thresholds are pre-registered (from bias_audit.md).**
   - Walling 5-10% email opt-in from relevant traffic = signal floor
   - 36% landing-to-install CR strongly validates UVP (PDF source)
   - <2% landing opt-in across 1000+ visitors = KILL signal
   - TikTok: ONE viral video (≥50K views with build-this comments) = green light; daily posting for 14+ days without one = revise hook or kill format
   - Reddit / X: detailed-excitement replies > 40% = green; negative/confused > 40% = kill

12. **Anti-AI-slop discipline is non-negotiable (item 31).** Every smoke test artifact ships with AI-codegen defaults UNLESS the founder explicitly counters. The validation artifact's failure mode CANNOT be "the smoke landing looked AI-generated." Per .agent/feedback memory: forbid Inter / indigo-500 / purple-blue gradient / rounded-xl-everywhere / Lucide trinity / emoji bullets. Use the OKLCH tokens + typography from emotional-design v2 `emotional_spec.md` if it has been run. If not, default anti-slop stack: Söhne or Cabinet Grotesk + ember #E36B2C.

13. **Solo-dev calibration.** Smoke landing setup: 1-2 hours (Carrd) or 4-8 hours (Lovable + custom). Traffic campaign: 2 weeks for statistical signal. Content-first TikTok track: 14-30 days minimum for algorithmic classification + organic reach. Stage 04 fits within Walling Stage 2 20-hour budget but Content-First track requires extended calendar time.

14. **2026 currency.** Lovable ($200M ARR Dec 2025). Bolt.new + v0.dev mid-2026 mainstream. RevenueCat State of Subs 2025/2026. Adapty 2026. FTC Click-to-Cancel (2025-07-14) — implications for "Coming Soon" subscription-style smoke checkouts. EU DFA Q4 2026 draft — fake urgency / confirm-shaming flagged. Apple Guideline 3.1.2 toggle-paywall ban 2026-01 (smoke paywall layout must comply already).

</core_principles>

Apply the decision matrix:

|---------------------|----------------------|

### BATCH 2 — Smoke landing brief (when Fake Door is primary)

**Wedge sentence (inherited from idea_landscape.md):** "[Leader] does X for general users; we do X for [segment] because [underservice]."

**Asset stack pick (item 31):**
- Carrd ($19/yr): fastest, simplest, hardest to make AI-slop (forces restraint)
- Lovable ($20/mo Pro): full-stack landing + light backend in <1 hour; HIGH anti-slop risk — counter explicitly
- Bolt.new + v0.dev: editable code, mid-control
- Unbounce / Instapage: paid-ads A/B-test infrastructure

**Anti-AI-slop discipline (mandatory, item 31):**
- Typography: Söhne, Cabinet Grotesk, Inter Tight (NOT Inter), system-ui — choose ONE distinctive heading + ONE body. Forbid: Inter, system-default "looks like every Lovable export."
- Accent color: ONE bold accent. Default suggestions: ember #E36B2C, olive #6E7F3F, vintage-red #C7434C. Forbid: indigo-500, purple-blue gradient.
- Icons: custom SVG OR Phosphor OR Tabler. Forbid: Lucide (the AI-codegen default trinity).
- Layout: NO rounded-xl-everywhere. Vary corner radii (some sharp, some pill-shaped). Forbid: emoji bullets.
- Photography: real or NO photo. Forbid: AI-generated hero illustration, generic stock figure-handshake.
- 925studios research: AI-slop landings 91% lower CR. Distinctive craft IS the smoke test's protection against false-negative.

**Landing structure (one-promise one-CTA):**
- Headline: ≤7 words, mentions specific underservice from wedge
- Subhead: names underserved segment + the underservice
- ONE primary CTA: "Buy now" OR "Reserve for $5" OR "Email me when ready" (in order of signal strength)
- Forbid: testimonial section, feature grid, FAQ accordion, pricing comparison table, hero stock illustration, social proof badges
- Total length: 1 viewport on mobile, ≤2 viewports on desktop

**"Buy Now" redirection / Coming Soon modal (item 33):**
- Click CTA → modal: "We're in R&D. Drop your email for the launch link" OR Stripe Payment Link for refundable deposit ($5-$25) OR Levels-style real-card-then-refund (hands off to Stage 05 PayCraft)
- Modal copy is sincere, not coy. "Currently in R&D" works; "Coming Soon (for real this time!)" does NOT.

### BATCH 4 — Content-First TikTok track (Mode B or supplementary)

**Pre-conditions check:**
- Magic moment must be (a) visually interesting, (b) explainable in 3 words, (c) tied to fundamental human desire/pain — per item 35 triad
- If can't satisfy all three → TikTok virality engineering is not the right validation surface; revert to Fake Door + Reddit/X mix

**Account warm-up protocol (item 35):**
- Create fresh TikTok account
- For 3-7 days BEFORE first post: watch / like / comment / save in target niche
- Goal: signal to algorithm what niche to classify this account into
- Pushscroll / Cal AI / Mau Baron all used this warm-up

**Hook formula (item 35):**
- 3-word rule: explain the value in ≤3 words
- Pushscroll: "Phone interrupts you. Do pushups."
- Cal AI: "Snap food. Get calories."
- Photo AI: "AI photos of you."
- Forbid: marketing taglines, multi-clause descriptions

**Iteration discipline:**
- Post daily for 14-30 days
- Iterate HOOKS, not the product
- One viral video (≥50K views with build-this comments) = validation event
- Daily posting for 30 days without a hit = revise hook OR revise product positioning

**Day-Zero Demand Capture (item 36):**
- Moment a video pops → spin up Discord community + Tally waitlist within hours
- Capture peak emotional state — cooled-down users convert worse
- Pushscroll playbook: viral Monday → MVP on App Store by Friday
- Anti-pattern: treating waitlist email signups as commercial validation (<5% conversion per Lenny benchmark)

**Spark Ads / UGC pipeline (item 37):**
- After a winning organic hook surfaces, scale via Spark Ads (creator-sourced ads cut CPA ~50%)
- UGC pipeline: pay 10-30 small creators $50-200 each → bank 100+ assets → top 10% become Spark Ads
- Mau Baron pattern: 3-day AI app → ₹17L/mo (~$20K/mo) in 6 months
- Connects to onboarding-v2 ad-creative ↔ onboarding promise alignment
